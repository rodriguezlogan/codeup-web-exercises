National Parks:

Yellowstone National Park
Nearly 3,500-sq.-mile wilderness recreation area atop a volcanic hot spot.
Grand Canyon National Park:
Home to much of the immense Grand Canyon, with its layered bands of red rock revealing millions of years of geological history.
Acadia National Park
A 47,000-acre Atlantic coast recreation area, its landscape is marked by woodland, rocky beaches and glacier-scoured granite peaks such as Cadillac Mountain
Yosemite National Park
It’s famed for its giant, ancient sequoia trees, and for Tunnel View.
Glacier National Park
A 1,583-sq.-mi. wilderness area with glacier-carved peaks and valleys running to the Canadian border.
Bryce Canyon National Park
A sprawling reserve in southern Utah, is known for crimson-colored hoodoos.
Zion National Park
A southwest Utah nature preserve distinguished by Zion Canyon’s steep red cliffs.
Arches National Park
Known as the site of more than 2,000 natural sandstone arches
Grand Teton National Park
Encompasses the Teton mountain range, the 4,000-meter Grand Teton peak, and the valley known as Jackson Hole.
Rocky Mountain National Park
Encompasses protected mountains, forests and alpine tundra. It's known for the Trail Ridge Road and the Old Fall River Road.

Yellow Stone

  • It encompasses 3,472 square miles (2,221,766 acres) which makes it larger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined.
  • There are more than 10,000 hydrothermal features
  • It's home to more than 500 active geysers
  • There are approximately 1,000 miles of hiking trails in Yellowstone.

Grand Canyon

  • The Grand Canyon is bigger than the state of Rhode Island.
  • The Grand Canyon itself can influence the weather.
  • The Grand Canyon was carved over some 6 million years.
  • The most dangerous animal in the park is the rock squirrel.

Acadia

  • Acadia is the fifth-smallest national park by land area.
  • Acadia is among the top ten most visited, hosting about 3.3 million people in 2016
  • Acadia National Park includes about 49,000 acres including nearly 13,000 in conservation easements for private lands and 31,000 on Mount Desert Island.
  • The park also includes 2,400 acres on the Schoodic Peninsula and nearby islands, plus 2,900 acres on Isle au Haut.
